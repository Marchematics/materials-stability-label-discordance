# Significance Statement

Crystal-discovery benchmarks commonly convert DFT energies above hull into
binary stable/unstable labels. This manuscript shows that those labels are
useful but not always source-invariant: in 43,139 strict Materials
Project--Alexandria matched structures, 11.7% of source-native binary labels
disagree. The study further shows that label-source choice changes benchmark
readouts under fixed predictions, including measurable precision@K shifts in a
CHGNet ranking diagnostic.

The contribution is a benchmark-reliability framework rather than a new model:
materials-discovery benchmark reports should disclose label source, hull
reference convention, source-conflict burden, near-threshold burden and
conflict-excluded sensitivity alongside binary stability endpoints.
