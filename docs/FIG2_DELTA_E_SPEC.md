# Figure 2 specification: cross-source energy-difference structure

## One-sentence claim

MP-vs-Alex stability-label discordance is enriched when the two sources assign substantially different energies above hull, but large `|Delta e|` is a risk factor rather than a sufficient condition for discordance.

## Why this replaces the original boundary-localization figure

The original `either-source near-hull` definition,

```text
min(abs(e_hull_MP), abs(e_hull_Alex)) <= threshold
```

is useful as a conservative reporting flag, but it is not independent evidence of localization. With binary labels defined by `e_above_hull <= 0`, a discordant pair necessarily has one source on the stable side of the threshold. In these matched tables, stable `e_above_hull` values are clipped at zero, so every discordant pair has `min(abs(e_hull_MP), abs(e_hull_Alex)) = 0`.

Therefore:

- use `|Delta e| = |e_hull_MP - e_hull_Alex|` as the main empirical axis in Fig. 2;
- move the either-source band to the benchmark-reporting calibration table;
- explicitly state that the band is an uncertainty flag, not the proof of the physical mechanism.

## Figure layout

Use a two-panel figure.

### Fig. 2a: discordance fraction by `|Delta e|` bin

Role: claim-supporting evidence.

X axis: `|Delta e|` in meV/atom.

Y axis: MP-vs-Alex binary discordance fraction.

Bins:

```text
[0,5), [5,10), [10,25), [25,50), [50,100), >=100 meV/atom
```

Draw exact binomial 95% confidence intervals and a dashed horizontal baseline at `31/287 = 10.8%`.

### Fig. 2b: distribution of `|Delta e|` for concordant and discordant pairs

Role: mechanism/context panel.

Plot a stacked or overlaid histogram using the same bins as panel a:

- concordant pairs in light gray;
- discordant pairs in the highlight color used in panel a.

This panel prevents overclaiming: most pairs have very small cross-source energy differences, discordant pairs are shifted upward, and the few largest `|Delta e|` outliers are still concordant because both sources remain on the same side of the hull threshold.

## Boundary flag calibration for later sections

Keep the `either-source` 25 meV result, but move it out of the main evidence role.

At 25 meV:

| definition | flag n | flag fraction | discordant captured | discordance among flagged | outside discordance |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| either-source min abs | 213 | 0.742 | 31/31 | 0.146 | 0/74 |
| both-sources max abs | 181 | 0.631 | 22/31 | 0.122 | 9/106 |
| MP reference abs | 198 | 0.690 | 29/31 | 0.146 | 2/89 |
| Alexandria reference abs | 196 | 0.683 | 24/31 | 0.122 | 7/91 |
| mean-source abs | 194 | 0.676 | 29/31 | 0.149 | 2/93 |

Operational wording:

> In the matched public-table representation, stable entries are reported at exactly `e_hull = 0` for both sources. Conditional on that clipped representation, any binary discordance triggers the either-source positive-threshold flag. At 25 meV/atom, the flag captures all 31 observed discordant pairs, but this sensitivity comes at a substantial flagging cost: it marks 74.2% of the matched population, of which 85.4% are concordant. Thus the flag is best interpreted as a conservative uncertainty annotation rather than a claim that all flagged structures are likely to disagree.

Note that a 10 meV either-source flag also captures all observed discordant pairs in this clipped `e_above_hull` table while flagging 57.5% of the population. This should be considered if the paper wants a less expensive operational recommendation than 25 meV.

## Structural localization supplement

The formula and element-family checks should be supplementary, not a main figure. Formula/prototype counts are too sparse for strong localization claims. Element-family membership is overlapping and shows no clean chemistry-specific failure mode:

- chalcogen-containing entries: 6/82 discordant, 7.3%;
- lanthanide/actinide-containing entries: 5/72 discordant, 6.9%.

Recommended sentence:

from pathlib import Path

import matplotlib.pyplot as plt
import pandas as pd

ROOT = Path(__file__).resolve().parents[1]
OUT = ROOT / "outputs" / "milestones" / "materials_label_discordance_preregistration"

bins = pd.read_csv(OUT / "table_fig2_delta_e_binned_discordance.csv")

    {
        "font.family": "sans-serif",
        "font.sans-serif": ["Arial"],
        "svg.fonttype": "none",
        "axes.spines.right": False,

fig, axes = plt.subplots(1, 2, figsize=(7.2, 3.0), constrained_layout=True)

x = range(len(bins))
y = bins["discordance_rate"]
axes[0].bar(x, y, color="#4C78A8", width=0.72)
axes[0].errorbar(
    x,
    y,
    yerr=[y - bins["ci95_lo"], bins["ci95_hi"] - y],

    ecolor="black",
    capsize=3,
    linewidth=1,

for i, row in enumerate(bins.itertuples(index=False)):
    axes[0].text(i, row.ci95_hi + 0.015, f"n={row.n}", ha="center", va="bottom", fontsize=8)

axes[1].bar(x, bins["concordant_n"], color="#C7C7C7", width=0.72, label="Concordant")

    x,

axes[1].set_xticks(list(x), bins["abs_delta_e_bin_mev"], rotation=35, ha="right")
axes[1].set_xlabel("|Delta e| (meV/atom)")

fig.savefig(OUT / "fig2_delta_e_discordance.png", dpi=450)

## Matching Results subsection

Recommended title:

```text
Cross-source energy differences, not boundary flags, explain discordance risk
```

Ready-to-edit topic paragraph:

```text
We next asked whether binary label discordance is associated with the
underlying real-valued disagreement between the two DFT sources, rather
than with a near-hull flag that is mathematically coupled to the label
definition. For each matched prototype we computed Delta e =
e_hull^MP - e_hull^Alex and stratified the pairwise discordance rate by
|Delta e|. Discordance was rare when the two sources agreed to within
10 meV/atom (11/218, 5.0%; exact binomial 95% CI, 2.5-8.8%), but rose
to 31.3% (20/64; 95% CI, 20.2-44.1%) for structures with
10 <= |Delta e| < 100 meV/atom. The largest |Delta e| bin was sparse
and contained only concordant same-side outliers (0/5), confirming that
cross-source energy separation is a risk factor for threshold crossing,
not a sufficient condition. Thus the empirical signal is best described
as a cross-source energy-difference tail that becomes consequential
when it straddles the stability threshold.
```
