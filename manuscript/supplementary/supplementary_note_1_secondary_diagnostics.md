# Supplementary Note 1. Secondary selection-conditioned diagnostics

This note records diagnostics that are useful for scope control but are not used
as source-wide estimates or as evidence for model behavior on the 43,139-structure
Materials Project--Alexandria denominator.

## Earlier 287-structure scored subset

ALIGNN-FF, CHGNet and MACE-MP raw scores were available only for an earlier
287-structure scored diagnostic subset. The three-model subset results are
superseded for benchmark-impact claims by the 5,000-structure CHGNet sensitivity
check in the main text. They remain useful only as a negative raw-score diagnostic:
top-decile discordance was 5/29 for ALIGNN-FF, 3/29 for CHGNet and 3/29 for
MACE-MP, and rank-permutation tests for the raw-score percentile coefficient were
non-significant (p=0.633, 0.367 and 0.327, respectively). These results should
not be interpreted as model rankings.

## WBM--Alexandria archived probe

An archived WBM--Alexandria probe is retained to explain why a previous
high-discordance observation should not be read as a generic DFT-label
discordance rate. The WBM probe gives 52.2% discordance on its own
release-conditioned denominator (141/270), whereas the full MP--Alexandria
MP-ID denominator gives 11.7%. Applying a simple MP-native exact-stable
selection to the full MP--Alexandria denominator raises discordance to 21.5%
(3,628/16,872), and an MP-native e_hull <= 25 meV atom^-1 sensitivity set gives
17.1% (4,677/27,280). These comparisons support a limited conclusion: source
pair, denominator and selection condition must be reported together.
